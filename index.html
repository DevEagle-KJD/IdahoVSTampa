How the comfort score works (higher = better)

A perfect 100 means dry, comfortable, and not muggy — the kind of air North Idaho is known for. The score blends three things:

  • Humidity (50%) — anything at or below 30% is perfect. It gets worse as humidity climbs toward 90%+.
  • Real feel (30%) — the "feels like" apparent temperature. Comfortable is ~50–72°F; extreme heat or cold drops the score.
  • Mugginess (20%) — sunshine times humidity. Sunny + dry is fine; sunny + humid is oppressive and gets punished hard.
