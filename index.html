How the comfort score works (higher = better)

A perfect 100 means dry, comfortable air — the kind North Idaho is known for. We deliberately don't score on raw humidity, because it lies: 75% humidity on a crisp 59°F morning feels fantastic, not muggy. Instead the score is built from two honest measures:

  • Air dryness — dew point (60%) — dew point is the actual moisture in the air, so it tracks how "sticky" it feels regardless of temperature. At or below 50°F the air is dry and crisp (full marks); by 70°F it's tropical and oppressive (zero). This is why a hot-but-dry day still scores well — low humidity means a low dew point.
  • Real-feel comfort — in the shade (40%) — the "feels like" temperature measured out of direct sun, because dry heat in the shade is far more bearable than the same number baking in the sun. Comfortable is ~55–82°F; extreme heat or cold drops the score.

Each card shows both real-feel numbers — sun (a sunny spot) and shade (a shady spot) — plus the dew point. The score uses the shade value. Data refreshes live from Open-Meteo every time this page loads — no login, no cost.
